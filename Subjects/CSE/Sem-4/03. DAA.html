B.Tech. - Semester 4


Design and Analysis of Algorithms

1. Basic Principles of Algorithm Design and Analysis
2. The Divide and Conquer Method: Overall technique, mergesort, quicksort, quickselect, FFT, etc.
3. The Greedy Method: Overall technique, the knapsack problem, optimal merge pattern, Huffman coding, minimum spanning tree, single-sourse shortest paths problem, etc.
4. Dynamic Programming: Overall technique, matrix chain problem, all-pairs shortest path problem, optimal binary search trees, etc.
5. Graph Traversal Techniques: Tree traversal and applications, depth-dirst search, bread-first search, connectivity algorithms, biconnectivity algorithms, etc.
6. Backtracking: Overall technique, generation of combinatorial objects such as graphs, sets, permutations, graph colorings, cliques, Hamiltonian cycles, etc.
7. Branch and Bound method: Overall method, the 0/1 knapsack problem, the job assignment problem, the traveling salesman problem, etc
8. Lower bound theory: Techniques for determining complexity lower bounds of problems, algorithm modeling, application to lower bound on sorting, searching, and merging.
9. Introduction to the Theory of NP-completeness: Nondeterministic algorithms, complexity classes, NPcompleteness, problem reduction, Specific NP-complete problems.
Recommended books:
1. Introduction to Algorithms by Cormen, leiserson and Rivest, McGraw-Hill, Latest Edition.
2. Design and Analysis of Algorithms by SartajSahni and Ellis Horwitz, Galgotia Publications.
3. Design and Analysis of Algorithms by Ullman and Hopcroft, Pearson Education.
